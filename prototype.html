Prototype Research

We did research on our prototype to find one that met our design constraints (time, information, skills, cost, tools, and manpower). We each made our own design for the prototype, listed here:

  1. Joe Mack's Ideas:
    1. A divide recycling box that has, on each of the recycled material, a guide to what can be recycled and how to recycle the objects, such as not having metal bottle baps on plastic bottles and not having leftover food in containers.
    2. Inverted bumps or holes instead of braille.
  2. Denalyn Miller's Ideas:
    1. Multiple bins each with their own color, symbols, and words indicating what goes inside.
    2. Normal rectangular bins stacked in rows.
    3. Circular bins on a rotating pedestal.
    4. Stickers to place on bins yourself which are customizable.
    5. Mat which will go under the bins which indicate what goes inside.
  3. Alexander Epolite's Ideas:
    1. Having several recycling bins that separate recyclable materials to reduce ambiguity.
    2. Slots which will hold labels that will rest on top of the recycling bin.

Top Design

After we made a decision-making matrix, we decided that the best prototype was the slot labels on the top of the bins.

A plastic board that has a slot for a printed paper which can be inserted for instructions on which recyclable material (such as cardboard, plastic, paper, etc.) goes in which bin. This board will also have instructions on how to properly recycle items, and will be attached to the top of the recycling bin.

Prototype Testing

We tested our prototype design against our design constraints. The resources used in this prototype was some leftover cardboard, a piece of paper, scissors, tape, and part of a plastic bag.

In this prototype, you can slot the paper in to indicate what types of recycling are allowed in the bin, and can easily be entered in either by hand or through a printed out paper.

Unfortunately, we did not have a great recycling bin to test our prototype on; however, this would work as an add-on for nearly any time of recycling bin used in schools and companies, as the footprint of these environments is much heavier than individuals.
